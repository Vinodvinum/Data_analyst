Project 1: Sales Performance Drop

Problem statement: Revenue fell 18% last month in one region. Leadership wants root causes and action plan.

Dataset description: Orders (date, region, product, quantity, price, discount), customers (segment), products (category, margin).

Excel steps

1) Clean blanks and duplicates. 2) Pivot by month/region/product. 3) Add MoM % formula. 4) Highlight biggest declines.

SQL steps

1) Aggregate revenue by month and region. 2) Compare current vs previous month. 3) Drill into product and segment drivers.

Python steps

1) Load and type-fix data. 2) Feature engineering for margin and discount rate. 3) Groupby trend and driver contribution.

Power BI steps

1) Star schema model. 2) DAX for Sales, Margin, MoM %. 3) Dashboard with KPI cards, trend, and product waterfall.

Final insights: Drop was concentrated in two products and one channel; discounting increased while volume stayed flat.

Business recommendation: adjust discount policy, run targeted campaign for high-margin products, and monitor weekly recovery KPI.

Project 2: Customer Churn Analysis

Problem statement: Many users stop using the product after signup. Product team needs churn causes by segment.

Dataset description: Users (signup date, segment), activity events (login, feature usage), subscriptions, support tickets.

Excel steps

1) Build cohort month. 2) Pivot active users by month offset. 3) Compute retention % and churn %.

SQL steps

1) First activity per user. 2) Cohort + activity month table. 3) Retention matrix by cohort and month.

Python steps

1) Create churn label. 2) Segment retained vs churned behavior. 3) Identify early warning indicators.

Power BI steps

1) Build retention dashboard. 2) DAX for retention and churn rate. 3) Segment slicers for root-cause view.

Final insights: Churn peaked in first 14 days for one segment with low feature adoption.

Business recommendation: redesign onboarding, trigger activation nudges, and launch support outreach for at-risk users.

Project 3: Finance Collections Dashboard

Problem statement: Collections are behind budget and overdue invoices are rising. Finance wants account-level action priorities.

Dataset description: Invoices (issue date, due date, amount), payments (payment date, amount), customers (risk tier), monthly budget.

Excel steps

1) Create aging buckets (0-30, 31-60, 61+). 2) Pivot overdue by customer tier. 3) Variance vs budget table.

SQL steps

1) Join invoices and payments. 2) Compute outstanding and delay days. 3) Rank high-value overdue accounts.

Python steps

1) Build payment-delay features. 2) Segment by risk and amount. 3) Weekly trend of recovery rate.

Power BI steps

1) Collections KPI model. 2) DAX for overdue %, recovery %. 3) Dashboard with aging, budget variance, and account priority.

Final insights: A small set of high-value accounts drove most overdue balance; delay pattern repeated in one risk tier.

Business recommendation: prioritize top overdue accounts, enforce escalation rules by tier, and monitor weekly recovery dashboard.
